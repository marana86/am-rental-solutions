10 Moving Tips for Inland Empire Families

Whether you're moving across Rancho Cucamonga or relocating from Ontario to Upland, these practical tips will help your move go smoother, faster, and with a lot less stress.

By A&M Rental Solutions June 2026 7 min read

We've helped hundreds of Inland Empire families move — and over time, we've noticed the same patterns. The moves that go smoothly all have a few things in common. The ones that don't, almost always skipped the same steps.

Here are 10 tips straight from the people who see move day up close every week.

1

Start Earlier Than You Think You Need To

Most people underestimate how long packing takes by 2–3x. If you think it'll take a weekend, plan for two. Start with rooms you rarely use — guest rooms, storage areas, holiday decorations — at least two weeks before your move date.

2

Move on a Weekday If You Can

In the Inland Empire, weekend traffic on the 210, 15, and 60 freeways can add an hour or more to a local move. If your schedule allows, a Tuesday or Wednesday move means less traffic, easier parking, and moving companies often charge less mid-week.

3

Beat the Heat — Start Before 8am

Inland Empire summers are brutal. Temperatures in Rancho Cucamonga, Fontana, and Ontario regularly hit 100°F+ in July and August. Start loading before 8am, take a midday break if needed, and have plenty of water on hand. Moving in the heat is exhausting and dangerous — don't underestimate it.

4

Label Everything — Including the Room It Goes To

Write the destination room on every tote in addition to the contents. "Kitchen — dishes" is much faster to unload than reading every label from scratch. Use a consistent system: a colored sticker per room works great with totes since you can see the label from across the truck.

5

Pack Heaviest Items in Smaller Totes

Books, tools, canned goods, and dishware all go in smaller or lighter-loaded totes. Totes are stackable, but a 60-lb tote is a back injury waiting to happen. Keep individual totes under 40 lbs and you'll move a lot faster — and safer.

6

Use Your Moving Dolly for Everything You Can

Our moving dolly isn't just for appliances — use it for stacks of totes, heavy furniture, bookshelves, and boxes. Moving 5 totes at once on a dolly beats five separate trips every time. It will cut your loading time in half.

7

Disconnect Appliances the Day Before

Washer and dryer connections, refrigerator water lines, and dishwasher hookups take time. Do all of this the day before so move day isn't delayed by plumbing tasks. Let your fridge defrost overnight with the doors open if it needs it.

8

Set Up Your New Home Before You Move In

If possible, do a walkthrough of your new place a day or two before the move. Check that all utilities are on, do a quick clean, and figure out where major furniture pieces will go. Making decisions about furniture placement while movers are standing there waiting costs money and time.

9

Keep an Essentials Tote Separate

Pack one tote with everything you'll need the first night: phone chargers, toiletries, a change of clothes, bedding, medications, snacks, and important documents. Mark it clearly and load it last so it comes off the truck first. The alternative — digging through 75 totes at 9pm for your toothbrush — is not fun.

10

Update Your Address Early

Start your USPS mail forwarding request online at least 2 weeks before your move date. Update your address with your bank, employer, insurance providers, and the DMV (California requires address updates within 10 days of moving). It takes longer than you expect and missing mail during a move creates headaches that last months.

📍 Local note: If you're moving within Rancho Cucamonga, note that many neighborhoods have HOA rules about moving truck parking and hours. Check with your new HOA before move day — some require permits for large trucks or restrict moving to certain hours.
