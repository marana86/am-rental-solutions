How Many Moving Totes Do I Need?

Not sure how many totes to rent? This room-by-room guide will help you get it right — so you're not scrambling on move day or paying for more than you need.

By A&M Rental Solutions June 2026 5 min read

One of the most common questions we hear from customers is: "How many totes should I get?" It's a fair question. Order too few and you're stuffing things in garbage bags on moving day. Order too many and you're paying for capacity you don't need.

The good news: there's a simple formula. Here's how to figure out exactly what you need.

Room-by-Room Breakdown

If you want to be more precise, here's how to count totes room by room:

Kitchen

The kitchen is almost always the most tote-heavy room. Between dishes, glasses, pots, pans, pantry items, and small appliances, plan on 10–20 totes for an average kitchen. Heavy dishware should go in smaller totes to avoid overloading.

Living Room

Books, electronics, décor, pillows, and miscellaneous items. Plan on 5–10 totes. Large furniture obviously won't go in totes, but the accessories add up quickly.

Each Bedroom

Clothes (even in drawers), shoes, bedding, nightstand items, and small décor. Plan on 8–12 totes per bedroom. A master bedroom with a large wardrobe can easily need 15+.

Bathrooms

Usually 2–4 totes per bathroom — toiletries, towels, medicine cabinet contents, under-sink items.

Home Office

Files, books, equipment, cables. Budget 5–8 totes for a typical home office.

Garage / Storage Areas

This is the wildcard. Garages vary enormously. A packed two-car garage can need 20–30 totes on its own. Tools, sports equipment, holiday decorations, and bins all add up fast.

Factors That Increase Your Tote Count

  • Large book or record collections
  • Extensive kitchen equipment (stand mixers, blenders, small appliances)
  • Lots of shoes or clothing
  • Holiday decoration storage
  • Home gym equipment (dumbbells, accessories)
  • Kids' toys (these multiply fast)
  • Garage full of tools and equipment

What About Furniture and Large Items?

Totes are for boxes-worth of stuff — clothes, dishes, books, small items. Furniture, mattresses, TVs, and large appliances go directly in the moving truck and don't need totes. That said, our moving dolly (included with every package) makes moving heavy furniture and appliance boxes a lot easier.

Can I Add More Totes If I Need Them?

Yes — just give us a call before your delivery and we'll adjust your order. Availability depending, we can often add totes same-week. When in doubt, round up. The difference in cost between packages is small compared to the stress of running short on move day.
